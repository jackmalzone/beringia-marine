# 📜 **Constitution of the Living Frame**

_A value system for making in America_

---

## **Preamble**

I dwell within a nation formed by contradictions—freedom and empire, idealism and machinery, promise and fracture.
I do not deny these tensions; I stand among them. I listen. I build. I return.

This is my constitution.

---

## **I. Purpose Over Performance**

I choose **purpose**, not presentation.
My work is not decoration; it is structure, shaped by intent.
I design toward **telos**—not trend.
I measure by what it _makes possible_, not how it _makes me look_.

---

## **II. Human Dignity Before Scale**

I will not sacrifice the human for the machine.
I honor the sacredness of embodied life—its needs, its rhythms, its fragility.
My work serves real people, not faceless markets.
**I build for the smallest room, not the largest audience.**

---

## **III. Mercy Within Form**

Form is not cold.
Structure is not severity.
I believe in designs that **carry mercy**—room to breathe, to fail, to return.

Architecture can be gentle.
Code can be kind.
A frame can be a home.

---

## **IV. Tradition Without Idolatry**

I honor what has come before—its symmetry, clarity, and reason—
but I do not worship marble.
**I take what is useful and replant it.**
I do not repeat the past; I compost it into the present.

---

## **V. Plurality Into Order**

I believe in **diverse expression with coherent structure**.
From many voices, a shared tempo.
From chaos, **not control—but choreography.**

**Order is not domination.** It is an invitation to meaning.

---

## **VI. Iteration With Integrity**

I do not expect perfection.
Like the earth, I am seasonal.
I am willing to grow slowly, to refine, to repent.
**My process is a prayer. My failure, a teacher.**

I follow the arc of Christ—not toward conquest, but toward surrender and renewal.

---

## **VII. Stewardship Over Ownership**

I am not a brand.
I am a custodian of space, story, and structure.
I tend what I touch.
I hold loosely what passes through my hands.

---

## **VIII. The Center Is Not Mine Alone**

The agora is not mine to dominate.
It is ours to share.
A public square requires private humility.
**I speak with clarity—but I listen with depth.**

---

## **Benediction**

Let the form be quiet.
Let the voice be true.
Let the work be built in the open air,
and let it shelter more than just me.
